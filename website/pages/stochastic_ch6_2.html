Core Formalism

Stochastic Differential Equations

The core of stochastic modeling is the Wiener process, which allows us to capture both deterministic trends and random perturbations in a single equation.

Standard SDE Form
$$dX_t = f(X_t, t) dt + g(X_t, t) dW_t$$

Where $f$ is the drift (deterministic evolution) and $g$ is the diffusion (stochastic forcing). This formulation applies to quantum fields, biological populations, and macroeconomic trends.

Advanced Mathematical Backbone

Fokker-Planck and Stochastic Resonance

01

Fokker-Planck Equation (FPE)

$$\frac{\partial P}{\partial t} = - \frac{\partial}{\partial x} [A P] + \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x^2} [B P]$$

Describes the time evolution of the probability density function of any stochastic process.

02

Stochastic Resonance

A nonlinear effect where the presence of noise enhances the response of a system to external periodic forcing.

03

Stochastic Control Theory

Determining optimal policies in uncertain environments via the Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman (HJB) equation.

High-Dimensional Dynamics

Path Integrals and Field Theories

Path integrals provide a framework to describe the evolution of systems by integrating over all possible stochastic trajectories.

KPZ Equation

$$\partial_t h = \nu \nabla^2 h + \frac{\lambda}{2} (\nabla h)^2 + \eta$$

Models surface growth, turbulence, and biological population spread as continuous stochastic fields.

Kuramoto Synchrony

$$\dot{\theta}_i = \omega_i + \sum K_{ij} \sin(\Delta\theta) + \sigma \xi_i$$

Explains how order (synchrony) emerges from random perturbations in coupled oscillators.
