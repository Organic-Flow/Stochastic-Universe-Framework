Stochastic Universe Section 5.2 Formal Mathematics

Mathematical Framework of SPL
The Calculus of Contradiction

Formalizing paradox through stochastic differential equations and the convergence of truth-value distributions.

Formal Definition

The Stochastic Logic Tuple

We define Stochastic Paradoxical Logic as a formal structure that extends classical valuation to handle temporal stochasticity and internal contradiction.

We define Stochastic Paradoxical Logic as a tuple $\mathcal{L} = \langle \mathcal{P}, \mathcal{V}, \mathcal{O}, \mathcal{R}, \mathcal{S} \rangle$ where:

$\mathcal{L} = \langle \mathcal{P}, \mathcal{V}, \mathcal{O}, \mathcal{R}, \mathcal{S} \rangle$

P

A set of propositions within the system.

V

Valuation function $\mathcal{V}: \mathcal{P} \rightarrow [0,1]$ assigning probability values.

O

A set of logical operators extended to handle probabilistic contradictions.

R

A set of inference rules that preserve probabilistic consistency.

S

A stochastic process governing the evolution of truth values over time.

Valuation

Truth Value Assignment

For any proposition $p \in \mathcal{P}$, its truth value $v(p)$ is a weighted composite of classical probability, paradoxical contribution, and real-time stochastic noise.

$$v(p) = \alpha \cdot P_{classical}(p) + \beta \cdot P_{paradox}(p, \neg p) + \gamma \cdot S_t(p)$$

where:

  • $P_{classical}(p)$ is the classical probability assignment
  • $P_{paradox}(p, \neg p)$ is the paradox contribution
  • $S_t(p)$ is the stochastic component at time $t$
  • $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ are weighting parameters with $\alpha + \beta + \gamma = 1$
Dynamics

Dynamic Evolution of Truth

The temporal evolution of truth values in SPL follows a stochastic differential equation where paradoxical tension drives the noise term.

Governing SDE
$$dP(p)_t = \left[\mu P(p)_t(1 - P(p)_t)\right]dt + \left[\sigma P_{paradox}(p, \neg p, t)\right]dW_t$$

where $\mu$ is the classical evolution rate, $\sigma$ is the paradox influence strength, $P_{paradox}(p, \neg p, t)$ encodes the stochastic contribution of paradoxical tension at time $t$, and $dW_t$ is a Wiener increment. Note that the noise coefficient absorbs the paradox influence: a proposition under greater internal contradiction experiences stronger stochastic forcing, preventing it from settling into a static binary state.

Equilibrium

Paradox Stability Conditions

A paradoxical system reaches stability when the expectation and variance of the truth-probability converge to a stationary equilibrium.

Expectation Convergence

$$\lim_{t \rightarrow \infty} \mathbb{E}[P(p,t)] = P_{equilibrium}(p)$$

Variance Convergence

$$\lim_{t \rightarrow \infty} \text{Var}[P(p,t)] = \sigma_{equilibrium}^2 < \infty$$
This equilibrium state represents the condition where paradoxes have stabilized into a self-sustaining configuration, which we propose corresponds to observable reality.
