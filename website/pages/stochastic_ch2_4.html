Ontological Distinction

Quantum vs. Classical Stochasticity

Classical: Emergent

Uncertainty stems from incomplete knowledge of initial conditions or thermal fluctuations. Randomness is an artifact of complexity (e.g., Brownian motion, turbulence, financial markets).

Quantum: Fundamental

Randomness is intrinsic to the wavefunction. It is not a result of ignorance but a feature of the universe's mathematical structure ($|\psi|^2$).

Unlike classical randomness, quantum stochasticity is not merely a result of ignorance but is deeply rooted in the mathematical structure of quantum theory.
